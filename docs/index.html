IoT Plug and play FAQs
Q. What can I do today that I couldn’t do before based on IoT Plug and Play?
A. IoT Plug and Play enables customers to connect IoT devices to the cloud without having to write a single line of embedded code, enabling IoT deployments to reach scale much faster. With such, businesses can capture the value of IoT to transform and expand their operational capabilities. IoT Plug and Play also enables our hardware partners to build IoT Plug and Play enable devices, which can then be certified with our Azure Certified for IoT program and used by customers and partners right away. This approach works with devices running any operating system, be it Linux, Android, Windows IoT, RTOSs, and more. And all our IoT Plug and Play support is open source as always. Visual Studio Code will support modeling an IoT Plug and Play device capability model as well as generating IoT device software based on that model, which dramatically accelerates IoT device software development. Finally, IoT Plug and Play bridge is a software component for Windows and Linux that allows sensors to just work with Azure by bridging them from the IoT gateway to IoT Central or IoT Hub.
Q. Which services from Azure IoT portfolio are integrated with IoT Plug and Play?
A. For public preview, IoT Plug and Play will be supported in Azure IoT Central and Azure IoT Hub
Q. How does IoT Plug and Play relate to Azure IoT Device Provisioning System (DPS)?
A. IoT Plug and Play focusses on modeling the capabilities of a device and how it should interact with IoT Hub or IoT Central. It does not address provisioning. That is still the role of DPS. They are highly complementary though. There is some overlap with the handling of data blobs as it relates to IoT Central but certified devices must support DPS and IoT Plug and Play.
